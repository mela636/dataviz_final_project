In the `project_01/` folder you can find a revised exploration of the **Comic Books Dataset (10,000 entries)** sourced from Kaggle (Rudra Kumar Gupta). The project analyzes publishing trends across countries, genres, and publishers through summary statistics and data visualizations. Japan leads in total published works with 3,575 comics, followed by the USA with 3,150. Page counts are heavily right-skewed, ratings cluster tightly between 7.5–9.0 across countries, and nearly 40% of comics received some form of award recognition. Publishing output peaked in 2023 before tapering off within the dataset.

## Project 02

In the `project_02/` folder you can find a revised exploration of two datasets from the Social Security Administration: the *Baby Names* dataset (1,924,665 observations, 1880–2017) and the *Beyond the Top 1000 Names* dataset (6,696,687 observations with state-level data). The motivation was to explore how baby name trends shift over time, how they differ by sex and geography, and how common my own name (*Melanie*) has been throughout history.

## Project 03

In the project 3, I utilized the Tampa International Airport weather data (2022) from FSU's Florida Climate Center and replicated several graphs regarding maximum temperatures, as well as a text analysis of the 2015 Billboard Top 100 lyrics. Visualizations include faceted histograms, density plots, ridgeline plots, a daily precipitation chart, and word clouds of the most common words found in song lyrics and titles.
